King Tutankhamen (colloquially known as King Tut) was born in 1341 B.C.E. to Pharaoh Akhenaten. Following his father’s death, King Tut became the Pharaoh of Egypt; he was nine years old. During his reign, Tut reserved many of his father’s decisions, including returning Egypt to polytheism. King Tut was buried in the Valley of the Kings following his death at the young age of nineteen. In 1922, archeologist Howard Carter discovered Tut’s tomb. It was incredibly well-preserved, which helped advance the field of Egyptology.
The King Tut Museum is located in Sharm El-Sheikh, Egypt, inside the Genena City Mall basement. The museum contains replicas of 130 pieces of King Tut’s jewelry, sculptures, and King Tut’s famous death mask. (The originals are in the Egyptian Museum in Cairo.) The King Tut Museum also has photographs and documents about the opening of King Tut’s tomb.

The White Star Line Shipping Company launched the luxury ocean-liner Titanic in 1912. At that time, the Titanic was the largest passenger ship in the world, and many considered her unsinkable. She began her maiden voyage from Southampton, England to New York City on April 10, 1912. At 11:40 midnight on April 14, the Titanic struck an iceberg, which flooded six of the Titanic’s sixteen watertight compartments and made it too heavy to float. The Titanic sank at 2:20 a.m. on April 15. Of the 2223 passengers on board, only 706 survived; an inadequate number of lifeboats exasperated the loss of life.

In 1985, oceanography Dr. Robert Ballard discovered the Titanic’s wreckage. Ironically, Ballard discovered the Titanic while on a secret mission to recover two American nuclear submarines. Ballard finished early and found the Titanic in the remaining time. The ship rests in the North Atlantic Ocean 12,000 feet below the surface.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klondike_Gold_Rush_National_Historical_Park
In 1896, Skookum Jim Mason, Tagish Charlie, and George Carmack struck gold in the Yukon Territory, Canada. This event sparked the Klondike Gold Rush, which lasted from 1897 until 1898. The Alaskan town of Skagway became a boomtown as thousands of prospectors rushed north to take the White Pass Trail to the Yukon Gold Fields. The stampeders contended with the cold, the rugged terrain, disease, and malnutrition as they hauled their equipment to the goldfields. While the White Pass Trail was only 35 miles long, prospectors had to make multiple trips, resulting in hundreds of miles walked on the trail. In 1898, the Klondike Gold Rush suddenly ended, sending Skagway into a decline. The Klondike Gold Rush National Park is located in Skagway, Alaska, and is now a popular stop for Alaskan cruises.

Boeing manufactured the Boeing VC-137B, or Special Air Mission (SAM) 970 in 1959 to serve as the first presidential jet. The SAM 970 is a specially built Boeing 707-120 with sophisticated radio equipment and a modified interior. Presidents Eisenhower, Kennedy, Johnson, and Nixon flew in it; this plane's (like all aircraft) callsign is Air Force 1 when the president is on-board. Besides presidents, other famous political figures like American Secretary of State Henry Kissinger and Soviet leader Nicholas Khrushchev rode in this plane. The Air Force replaced the SAM 970 in 172 with the upgraded Boeing VC-137C, though the SAM 970 remained in the presidential fleet carrying the Vice President and other influential figures until 1996.

The firm initially focused on auctioning fine art, period furniture, books, and porcelain. But, in the 1970s, Adam's began to sell Irish paintings, which at the time was a rapidly growing market.
